EHO Reporting SOP SOP Code: EHO/MSU/Surv/01
Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) Version: 1.0
Effective Date: 01-06-2025 Prepared By: EPID Cell, MSU Approved By: Assistant Municipal Commissioner (Health), Municipal Corporation of Greater Mumbai
1. Purpose

To define the standardized process for collection, compilation, cleaning, analysis, reporting and dissemination of disease surveillance data on the IHIP portal for EHO reporting.

2. Scope

This SOP applies to all facilities reporting on the IHIP portal and EPID Cell, MSU for data management, report generation and sharing.

3. Definitions
• S Form : Syndropic Surveillance Form (reported by Health post staff) • P Form : Presumptive Surveillance Form (reported by Doctors) • L Form : Laboratory Surveillance Form (reported when samples are taken for lab investigation) • MSU : Municipal Surveillance Unit • EHO : Executive Health Officer • AMO : Assistant Medical Officer • IPD : In-Patient Department • OPD : Out-Patient Department
4. Roles & Responsibilities
  • Facilities: Report cases on IHIP portal using S, P and L Forms.
  • MSU Data Manager: Download, compile, clean, analyze, maintain datasets and generate reports.
  • Surveillance AMO: Use reports for follow up and surveillance activities.
  • State/District Offices: Use shared data for surveillance and response.
5. Procedure
Step No. Procedure Step
1Facilities report patients with their demographic details and disease details on IHIP portal using their login credentials under S, P and L Forms.
2After reporting on IHIP portal, cases are available in S Form, P Form and L Form.
3At EPID Cell, MSU Data Manager downloads the required line lists from IHIP portal.
4 Data Compilation:
4.1 Download cases from P form (Diarrhoea and Dysentery cases) and read them as Gastro cases.
4.2 Download all disease cases from L Form.
4.3 Compile both datasets (Gastro + L Form all diseases) into one master line list.
5 Data Cleaning & Preparation:
5.1 Remove duplicate cases. A patient reported within 30 days for the same disease complaint is considered as duplicate and removed.
5.2 Allocate wards to every case in the line list according to the patient's address.
5.3 Extract out of Mumbai cases in separate file.
6Send the out of Mumbai cases Excel file to State Office and the respective District Surveillance Offices through email.
7Make a separate file for Malaria cases. Share this Malaria cases file and the master case line list (including Malaria cases) to all wards marked to Surveillance AMO for further follow up and surveillance activities.
8Maintain the master case line list Google Sheet which includes daily cases appended.
9Maintain the month-wise and disease-wise cases in the respective separate tabs in separate Google Sheet.
10Using the pivot table feature of Google Sheet prepare EHO reports (4 formats).
6. Reports (4 Formats)
Report No. Report Name Format Description
1 EHO Report In first column all diseases, from second column Jan to Dec (include half yearly total after Jun and Dec and add total in the last column).
2 Ward wise All Disease Format In first column all wards. From second column each consists Disease 1, Disease 2, Disease 3, ..., Disease n. This includes number of cases wardwise from 01 Jan to Till date.
3 Year wise Monsoon Disease Report In first row it includes Years from 2021 to 2026 (Till date). Each year has number of cases and deaths. Second row is disease name. Now make short table of each disease.
4 Ward wise Month wise Monsoon Disease Report Includes 3 tables:
1) Ward wise Month wise cases (Jan-25 to May-26)
2) Month wise IPD & OPD percentage to total month cases
3) Public & Private cases reporting percentage to total month cases
7. Data Sharing & Dissemination

Google Sheets in Step 6 and Step 7 are downloaded as Excel files and scheduled to be shared with stakeholders every next day at 8:00 AM through email.
