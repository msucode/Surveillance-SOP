SOP – Official Email Monitoring,
Register Entry & Follow-Up

Metropolitan Surveillance Unit (MSU), Mumbai

Email Duty Rotation
  • One staff member assigned for 01 month
  • Responsibility continues irrespective of Sundays, holidays or leave periods

Purpose: To ensure systematic monitoring, documentation, escalation, follow-up, and timely response of all official emails received by the Metropolitan Surveillance Unit (MSU). This SOP ensures proper maintenance of records, timely action, accountability and continuous monitoring without interruption.

1 1. Email Checking

Frequency:

  • Every Morning
  • Every Evening
  • Additional checks if urgent communication expected
2 2. Identify Important Emails

Include Emails Related To:

  • Surveillance reports
  • Outbreak alerts
  • Death reviews
  • Urgent government communication
  • Data requests
  • PPT/report submissions
  • Meeting instructions
  • Public health emergencies
  • State/National directives
  • Any other official important communication
3 3. Email Register Entry

Every important email must be entered in the Email Register.

Email Register Format
Date Time Sender Subject Action Required Assigned To Reply Sent Senior Signature
4 4. Print & Submit to Senior

The assigned staff shall:

  1. Take printout of important email
  2. Attach print with Email Register
  3. Submit to Senior Doctor/Officer

Senior Doctor shall:

  • Review communication
  • Give instructions
  • Sign register
  • Escalate matter if required
5 5. Reporting to Senior Staff

The duty staff must inform senior staff:

• Every morning • Every evening

Regarding:

  • Important emails received
  • Pending replies
  • Urgent actions required
  • Escalated matters
6 6. Follow-Up Responsibility

The assigned email duty staff is fully responsible for ensuring:

  • Reply email sent
  • Required report submitted
  • PPT shared
  • Data submitted
  • If meeting or webinar email is received, schedule it in email calender
  • Compliance completed within timeline

This responsibility continues until task completion.

7 7. Escalation

If required, Senior Doctor may inform:

  • Higher authorities
  • Senior administrative officers
  • Public health leadership

For urgent or important matters.

8 8. Handover System

After completion of 01 month duty period:

  • Proper handover must be done
  • Pending emails/tasks explained
  • Register handed over
  • Pending replies/status updated
9 9. If Staff is Unavailable

If assigned staff is:

  • On leave
  • Sick
  • Unavailable
➔

Then:

  • Immediate alternate staff must be assigned
  • Proper interim handover required
  • Senior staff should be informed immediately

Important

The assigned email duty staff is accountable for:

  1. Continuous email monitoring
  2. Register documentation
  3. Timely communication to seniors
  4. Ensuring replies/submissions are completed
  5. Proper follow-up till closure
  6. Smooth handover to next duty staff
