Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

Typhoid Active Case Search (ACS)

Metropolitan Surveillance Unit, BMC, Mumbai

1. Roles & Responsibilities
Role Key Responsibilities
Facility Staff
  • Identify suspected typhoid case
  • Inform Ward EPI Officials
  • Assist in sample collection
  • Conduct Active Case Search (ACS)
  • Collect field and environmental data
  • Collect samples of suspected cases and water sources
  • Register any additional positive cases
Ward EPI Officials
  • Fill Case Investigation Form (CIF)
  • Coordinate and ensure sample collection
  • Submit CIF and case details to F South Office
F South Office
  • Maintain suspected case Google Sheet
  • Generate Unique EPID Number for each suspected case
  • Copy case details to “Typhoid_Case_Mumbai” and share with Microbiology Laboratory
Microbiology Laboratory Nodal Officer
  1. Receive samples and perform laboratory testing.
  2. Update Google Sheet “Typhoid_Case_Mumbai” with the following details:
    • Date sample received
    • Specimen ID
    • Date of result
    • Laboratory result
    • Pathogen details (if positive)
  3. Maintain positive case line list Google Sheet “TYPHOID CASES - 2026”.
  4. Ensure timely updating of all laboratory-confirmed typhoid cases.
  5. Share selective operational details from each positive case with EPI/Surveillance/Ward AMOs through MSU Ward Coordinators.
  6. Coordinate with surveillance units regarding positive cases requiring field investigation.
  7. Maintain laboratory surveillance records and ensure data quality.
MSU Ward Coordinators
  • Receive positive case details.
  • Share positive case details with concerned EPI/Surveillance/Ward AMOs and see to that Active Case Search (ACS) and water sampling completed.
EPI / Surveillance / Ward AMOs
  • Assign positive cases to concerned health facilities based on patient residential address
  • Monitor field action and reporting
Facility / Field Staff
  • Conduct ACS in patient locality
  • Collect required field details
  • Collect water sample and send for testing
  • Register new positive cases and enter into surveillance system
2. Purpose

To detect typhoid cases early through active case search, identify clusters and potential outbreak areas, detect source of contamination and strengthen community awareness.

3. Scope

This SOP applies to all health facilities, Ward EPI Officials, F South Office, Microbiology Laboratory, MSU Ward Coordinators, EPI/Surveillance/Ward AMOs and field health staff involved in typhoid surveillance and active case search.

4. Workflow Steps
1
Suspected Case Identification
  • Health facility identifies suspected typhoid patient
  • Facility staff informs respective Ward EPI Officials
2
Case Investigation & Sample
  • Ward EPI Officials fill CIF
  • Coordinate sample collection and inform concerned staff
  • CIF and suspected case details submitted to F South Office
  • Patient samples sent to Microbiology Laboratory, Kasturba Hospital
3
Case Registration & EPID Generation
  • F South Office maintains suspected case Google Sheet
  • Generate Unique EPID Number for each suspected case
  • Copy case details to “Typhoid_Case_Mumbai” and share with Microbiology Laboratory
4
Laboratory Testing & Update
  • Microbiology Laboratory Nodal Officer updates: Date sample received, Specimen ID, Date of result, Laboratory result, Pathogen details (if positive)
  • Positive results entered into positive case line list maintained by Microbiology Laboratory Nodal Officer
5
Positive Case Line Listing
  • Microbiology Laboratory Nodal Officer maintains Google Sheet “TYPHOID CASES - 2026”
  • Share selective operational details from each positive case with EPI/Surveillance/Ward AMOs through MSU Ward Coordinators
6
Case Allocation to Facilities
  • EPI/Surveillance/Ward AMOs assign positive cases to concerned health facilities based on patient residential address
  • Facility staff receive assigned cases for field investigation
7
Active Case Search (ACS) & Field Visit
  • Facility staff conduct ACS in patient locality
  • Collect details: Date of ACS, households visited, suspected cases found, water source details, water sample collection and result, occupation of index case, admission/discharge dates, symptomatic/sick persons in family, neighbourhood or school
  • Assess possible clustering and source of contamination
8
Detection of Additional Positive Cases
  • If suspected cases identified during ACS, collect samples
  • Register cases and enter into surveillance system
  • Follow the same investigation and laboratory workflow
9
Surveillance & Outbreak Detection
  • Detect typhoid clusters
  • Identify outbreak-prone areas
  • Detect contaminated water sources
  • Monitor community transmission
  • Strengthen public health response
  • Improve community awareness
Abbreviations: EPI - Expanded Programme on Immunization  |  EPID - Epidemiology ID Number Unit  |  CIF - Case Investigation Form  |  ACS - Active Case Search  |  AMO - Assistant Medical Officer
