30–60 Minutes Daily

1 Step 1 – Check Google Form Responses (10 Min)

Time: Daily at 3:00 PM

Responsibility: Assigned MSU surveillance staff

Action:
  • Open Death CIF Google Form response sheet
  • Review all newly submitted entries received from Mumbai hospitals
2 Step 2 – Screen Reported Deaths (10–15 Min)
Include Only:
  • Infectious disease-related deaths
  • Suspected outbreak-related deaths
  • Fever deaths under investigation
  • Vector-borne diseases
  • Water-borne diseases
  • Viral infections
  • Respiratory infectious diseases
Exclude:
  • Tuberculosis (TB) deaths
  • Non-infectious deaths
  • Trauma/accidents
  • Poisoning
  • Chronic non-communicable diseases
3 Step 3 – Verify & Prepare Case Summary (5–10 Min)
Check:
• Patient Name • Date of Admission • Age/Sex • Date of Death • Address • Diagnosis/Cause of Death • Hospital Name • Ward Jurisdiction • Contact Details

Prepare short death summary for communication.

4 Step 4 – Inform Respective Ward (Most Important)

Share death details with:

• Medical Officer of Health (MOH) • Ward Nodal Officer • Surveillance Team

Mode of communication:

• WhatsApp • Official group • Email (if required)
Message Format

Death case reported through MSU Death CIF Google Form.

Disease:
Patient Name/Age/Sex:

Respective ward is requested to conduct field investigation, complete CIF, undertake necessary survey activities, and submit report to MSU urgently.

5 Step 5 – Field Investigation by Ward

Ward Responsibilities:

  1. Visit residence of deceased patient
  2. Conduct field investigation
  3. Complete CIF (Case Investigation Form)
  4. Collect epidemiological information
  5. Identify clustering/similar illness if present
  6. Conduct surrounding area survey if required
  7. Take necessary public health action
  8. Submit investigation report to MSU
6 Step 6 – Report Follow-Up

MSU staff should:

  • Track pending investigations
  • Ensure CIF/report submission
  • Maintain death investigation records
  • Escalate delayed cases if required
