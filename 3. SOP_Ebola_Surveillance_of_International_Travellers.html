Standard Operating Procedure (SOP)

SOP Monitoring of Passengers From Ebola Affected Countries

Review Date : _________________
Prepared By : _________________
Approved By : _________________
1. Purpose

To ensure systematic monitoring of passengers arriving from Ebola affected countries through daily telephonic surveillance for 21 days after arrival for early detection of Ebola Virus Disease and timely public health intervention.

2. Roles & Responsibilities

Nilesh / Data Management Staff

  • Receive passenger list from Airport Health Organization (APHO).
  • Remove duplicate records.
  • Place newly added passengers at top of the list.
  • Verify ward allocation and update master line-list.

Ward Co-ordinator

  • Conduct daily telephonic follow-up.
  • Ask required questions in given sequence.
  • Record symptoms and remarks.
  • Update Day 1 to Day 21 status.
  • Escalate symptomatic passenger as per protocol.

Medical Officer / Supervisor

  • Review symptomatic passengers.
  • Provide necessary guidance.
  • Ensure appropriate referral and management.
  • Monitor surveillance activities.
3. Key Definitions

APHO : Airport Health Organization

Active Surveillance : Daily telephonic monitoring of passengers for 21 days from the date of arrival.

Symptomatic Passenger : Passenger having fever, body ache, headache, weakness or any other symptom compatible with Ebola Virus Disease.

4. Surveillance Period

Active surveillance will be continued for 21 days from the date of arrival in Mumbai/India.

7. Management of Symptomatic Passengers
  • Immediately inform Medical Officer / Supervisor.
  • Follow Ebola Response Protocol.
  • Arrange further evaluation / referral as per guideline.
