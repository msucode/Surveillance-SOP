Food Borne Outbreak Investigation

Comprehensive Operational Flow Chart & Protocol Guidelines

1

Confirm the Outbreak

  • Verify that the number of cases exceeds expected levels for that area/time.
  • Review OPD/IPD line lists and laboratory reports.
  • Rule out duplicate reporting.
Outbreak Confirmed?
Yes No
*If No: Continue Routine Surveillance
2

Establish Case Definition & Activate RRT

Develop Standard Case Definition

(Suspected / Probable / Confirmed)

  • Person: Residents, school children, wedding attendees, etc.
  • Place: Specific BMC ward, society, chawl, hotel, mess, caterer.
  • Time: Onset within a defined period (e.g., last 24–72 hours).
  • Symptoms: Diarrhoea, vomiting, abdominal pain, fever, etc.
Activate Rapid Response Team (RRT)
  • Medical Officer
  • Epidemiologist
  • Health Inspector
  • Sanitary Inspector
  • Food Safety Officer (FSO)
  • Laboratory staff
3

Active Case Search & Line Listing

  • Visit private clinics, hospitals, nursing homes and house-to-house search.
  • Prepare a line list including:
    • Name, age, sex
    • Address & mobile number
    • Date/time of food consumption
    • Date/time of symptom onset
    • Symptoms, hospitalization status
    • Outcome (recovered/admitted/death)
4

Descriptive Epidemiology

TIME - PLACE - PERSON

  • Time: Construct an epidemic curve to identify point source vs. continuous exposure.
  • Place: Spot map cases by lane/building/society.
  • Person: Analyse by age group, gender, occupation, food consumed.

Insight: This helps identify the suspected food item and source.

Simultaneous Environmental Investigation
5

Hypothesis Generation

Based on interviews:

  • Identify common food exposures (wedding meal, street vendor, school mid-day meal, hotel).
Important Note:
  • Time gap between eating and illness (incubation period)
  • Food preparation/storage practices
  • Leftover food availability
6

Water & Food Safety Investigation

Joint Inspection
  • Medical Officer of Health (MOH)
  • Sanitary Inspector
  • Food Safety Expert
Key Checkpoints
  • Kitchen hygiene & cleanliness
  • Source of raw materials
  • Food storage temp
  • Cooking & reheating
  • Water source for cooking
  • Hand hygiene
  • Pest infestation
Immediate Actions
  • Seal food premises
  • Stop food distribution
  • Collect leftover food samples
7

Sample Collection & Lab Testing

Collect protocols for:
  • Stool samples: from cases (preferably early cases)
  • Food samples: (leftovers)
  • Water samples: (if water suspected)
Send Logistics:

Send to BMC - Municipal Analyst Laboratory. Always maintain the cold chain & ensure proper labelling.

8

Analytical Epidemiology

If source is unclear:

  • Conduct a retrospective cohort study (especially useful for common discrete events).

This methodology helps confirm the responsible food item definitively.

Source Identified? Yes → Destroy Source*

*Destroy source only after formal lab confirmation.

9

Implement Control & Preventive Measures

Execute Action Plan Without Waiting for Lab Confirmation

Treat Cases
ORS, IV fluids, Antibiotics if indicated
Health Education
Handwashing, Safe handling, Avoid street food
Chlorination
Of water sources if required
Medical Camps
Setup in heavily affected areas
Active Case Search
Continuous scanning in source areas
10

Risk Communication & Coordination

Inform
  • MOH
  • Assistant Medical Officer (AMO)
  • Ward Control Room
  • Public: Clear messaging to avoid panic.
Coordinate
  • Food Safety Dept
  • Education Dept (if school-related)
  • Police (if legal action required)
11

Documentation & Reporting

Prepare standard investigation report including:

  • Background & detection
  • Investigation Methods
  • Findings (epi curve, spot map, tables)
  • Control measures taken & Recommendations

Submission Channels:

Submit through IHIP (Form S/L) and official BMC communication portals.

12

Post-Outbreak Follow-Up

  • Monitor for new incident cases for a duration of 2 incubation periods.
  • Re-inspect food/water premises thoroughly before granting permission for reopening.
  • Conduct structured training of food handlers.
  • Strengthen routine healthcare surveillance networks within the ward.
13

Outbreak Review & Strengthening

  • Evaluate the effectiveness of the investigation and response timeline.
  • Support structural gap identification and prompt rectification protocols.
  • Strengthen general capacity building of frontline field staff.
  • Update localized contingency response plans if required based on lessons learned.
✓ OUTBREAK CLOSED
CRITICAL PROTOCOL MANDATE NOTE

All process phases and corresponding steps are to be documented properly. Timely action, multi-departmental coordination, clear risk communication, and robust laboratory support are essential to control the outbreak and prevent further secondary cases.

Ensure reporting of the event within one day and seamless completion of the early outbreak response framework within seven days.
